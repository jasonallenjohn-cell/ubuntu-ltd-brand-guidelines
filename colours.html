Ubuntu LTD · Brand · Colour Review For the team · 2026-05-20

Earth, sun,
and umber.

This is the proposed palette for Ubuntu Land Trust and Developments — and for everything we put out under the Common Ground Initiative banner. It is intentionally earthen: aged savannah cream, deep warm umber, and a single savannah-gold accent. No corporate blue, no ceremonial purple in the load-bearing 80%.

What we'd like your eyes on

  • Does the four-colour primary set feel right for a national, Black-led housing institution?
  • Is the umber dark enough to read as serious, without feeling heavy?
  • Is the gold the right warmth — or should it pull cooler / brighter?
  • Of the extended (staff) colours, are there any you would cut or add?
  • Anything you'd push us to reconsider before we apply this to the website, decks, and stationery?
01 · Primary · 80% of everything we publish

Four colours, load-bearing.

Aged savannah cream, warm umber, savannah gold, ink. Earthen — fired clay, deep loam, sun — not corporate blue. Eighty percent of everything we put out should use only these.
