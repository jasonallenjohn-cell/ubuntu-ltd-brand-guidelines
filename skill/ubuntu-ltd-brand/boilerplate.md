## 250 words

> Ubuntu Land Trust and Developments (Ubuntu LTD) is the national platform behind the Common Ground Initiative — a coordinated, repeatable model for delivering permanent affordable housing at scale across Canada.
>
> Ubuntu LTD does not acquire land for itself. Instead, it structures and coordinates a four-step mechanic: private landowners contribute entitled sites as preferred-equity contribution into project-level Special Purpose Vehicles; mission-aligned non-profit operators take ownership of the building entity on day one of occupancy, with no upfront capital required; construction is financed on institution-grade terms through the Canada Mortgage and Housing Corporation (CMHC) and Build Canada Homes (BCH); and at Year 20, twenty years of mortgage paydown together with the equity remaining in the deal cover a fair-value redemption for the landowner, while the non-profit acquires the land fee-simple. A clean exit for the contributor; a strong balance sheet for the operator going forward.
>
> The platform emerged from the African Canadian Affordable Housing (ACAH) Village — a CMHC-funded initiative led by Ubuntu LTD CEO Kizito Musabimana through the Rwandan Canadian Healing Centre. As of May 2026, the Common Ground Initiative pipeline stands at 5,131 homes across 18 entitled sites in the southern Ontario corridor, with a Launchpad cohort of 156 homes across four sites — 844 Veterans Drive (Barrie), Main Street (Listowel), Oakwood Avenue (Toronto), and 27 Blake Street (Toronto) — breaking ground in 2026.
>
> Ubuntu LTD is governed by a Black-led Board of Directors with deep experience in housing systems, refugee settlement, ecosystem leadership, and trusted community institutions.

## Common Ground Initiative (CGI) one-paragraph descriptions

### Short (50 words)
> The Common Ground Initiative is Ubuntu LTD's flagship programme — a repeatable four-step mechanic that turns entitled private land into permanent affordable housing without requiring landowners to sell, non-profit operators to bring upfront capital, or government funders to underwrite one-off deals.

### Long (140 words)
> The Common Ground Initiative (CGI) is the structured housing-delivery programme of Ubuntu Land Trust and Developments. Through CGI, private landowners contribute entitled sites as preferred-equity contribution into project-level Special Purpose Vehicles; mission-aligned non-profit operators own the buildings from day one of occupancy; CMHC and Build Canada Homes provide construction financing on institution-grade terms; and at Year 20, mortgage paydown together with retained equity fund a fair-value redemption for the landowner while the non-profit acquires the land fee-simple. The model is intentionally boring — repeatability is the feature. Every site follows the same four steps in the same order, so each successive deal is faster to close, easier to underwrite, and cleaner to govern. The CGI pipeline currently stands at 5,131 homes across 18 entitled sites in the southern Ontario corridor.

## "About the team" — short bio paragraph

> Ubuntu Land Trust and Developments is governed by a Black-led Board of Directors with deep experience in housing systems, refugee settlement, ecosystem leadership, and trusted community institutions. The executive team has built and operated affordable housing under CMHC-funded programmes. The Board includes Kemi Jacobs (former Director of Housing, Toronto Community Housing), Amanuel Melles (Executive Director, Network for the Advancement of Black Communities), and Nadine Miller (Executive Director, Pilgrim's Feast Tabernacle Church). Kizito Musabimana serves as Chief Executive Officer and Jonathan Okubay as Managing Director.

## Stock phrases — when these specific concepts come up, use this wording

- **Year-20 land purchase:** "Twenty years of mortgage paydown together with the equity remaining in the deal cover a fair price for the landowner and leave the non-profit owning both the building and the land beneath it. A clean exit for the contributor; a strong balance sheet for the operator going forward."

- **What Ubuntu LTD is:** "Ubuntu LTD is the platform that turns entitled private land into permanent affordable housing — coordinating landowners, non-profit operators, and federal capital around a single, repeatable model."

- **What Ubuntu LTD isn't:** "Ubuntu LTD does not acquire land for itself, hold a portfolio of properties as a fund, or operate as a real estate investment trust. It is a coordinating entity — the third party at the table."

- **Why now:** "Canada's affordable-housing shortfall is structural. The federal commitment is real and the capital is real — but the actual sites that need to be built on sit with private landowners. Common Ground is the structural bridge."

- **The team in one sentence:** "Black-led board and executive with thirty years in housing, ecosystem leadership, and trusted community institutions."
