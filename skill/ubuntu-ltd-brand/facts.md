## The four-step model (Common Ground Initiative mechanic)

**Step 01 — Land contributed as equity**
A private landowner contributes an entitled site into a project-level SPV as preferred-equity contribution. Not a sale, not a donation. Structured contribution with a defined redemption pathway at Year 20.

**Step 02 — Non-profit owns the building**
A mission-aligned non-profit operator takes ownership of the building entity on day one, no upfront capital required. Ubuntu LTD coordinates legal structure and operator selection. The operator runs the building from day one of occupancy.

**Step 03 — CMHC + Build Canada Homes finance**
Construction is financed on institution-grade terms through the Canada Mortgage and Housing Corporation (CMHC) and Build Canada Homes (BCH). Ubuntu LTD packages the financial model, governance attestations, and operator readiness materials.

**Step 04 — Year-20 land purchase**
Twenty years of mortgage paydown together with the equity remaining in the deal cover a fair price for the landowner and leave the non-profit owning both the building and the land beneath it. A clean exit for the contributor; a strong balance sheet for the operator going forward.
