# Voice — Ubuntu LTD

## Three voice principles

1. **Plain, precise, generous.** Short Anglo-Saxon words over long Latinate ones. Specific over abstract. Generous in describing partners; never preachy.
2. **Numbers carry the argument.** When a number can do the work, let it. "5,623 homes across 21 sites" is a sentence. Don't pad it.
3. **Institution, not start-up.** Ubuntu LTD speaks as an institution from day one. Not "we're going to build" — "we are building." Not "innovative platform" — "platform." Not "disrupting affordable housing" — "running the math."

## What we sound like

> Land contributed as equity is land that carries the math.

> The Common Ground Initiative closed the year with twenty-one entitled sites and 5,623 units of permanent affordable housing in pipeline across Canada.

> Non-profit operators own the buildings day one. The land transfers to them at Year 20.

> A clean exit for the contributor. A strong balance sheet for the operator going forward.

## What we don't sound like

> ❌ "We're a disruptive new platform revolutionizing affordable housing."
> ❌ "Our innovative model creates win-win outcomes for all stakeholders."
> ❌ "Ubuntu Land Trust will leverage best-in-class governance to unlock value."
> ❌ "We empower communities through transformative real estate solutions."

These are the corporate-aspirational register we reject. Read them and notice: no numbers, no specifics, no mechanism, no accountability.

## Do / don't word lists

### Do say
- **platform** (we are one)
- **coordinator / structuring entity / service provider** (what Ubuntu LTD is)
- **contributed** (land), **entitled** (site), **pipeline** (sites under binding instruments)
- **mechanic** (the four-step process), **repeatable** (the model)
- **carries the math** (substitute for "pencils out")
- **Year-20 land purchase** (the redemption event)
- **fee simple** (what the NFP gets at Year 20)
- **operator** (the non-profit that owns and runs the building)
- **landowner** (the private party contributing the parcel)
- **funder / capital partner** (CMHC, BCH, municipal contributors)

### Don't say
- **trust** (in the mutual-fund sense) — Ubuntu LTD is not a holding fund
- **acquire** (Ubuntu LTD doesn't acquire land for itself — it coordinates contribution into project SPVs)
- **disrupt / disruptive / disruption** — corporate placeholder
- **innovative / innovation / innovative platform** — same
- **revolutionize** — same
- **best-in-class / world-class / industry-leading** — same
- **stakeholders** — say "landowners, operators, funders" instead
- **win-win / synergies / leverage / unlock value** — corporate placeholder
- **going forward** (when used as filler) — only use it literally (e.g., "the operator owns the land going forward")
- **excited to announce** — name what's happening instead
- **at scale** (overused) — fine in moderation; the tagline uses it, don't repeat in every paragraph

## Vocabulary — definitions to use consistently

**Common Ground Initiative (CGI).** Ubuntu LTD's flagship programme. The four-step mechanic for turning entitled private land into permanent affordable housing. Repeatable across sites and regions.

**Entitled site.** A parcel of land that has cleared zoning, planning, and approval thresholds such that construction can begin once financing and contracts are in place. Not "raw land."

**Year-20 land purchase.** The redemption event in the Common Ground mechanic. Twenty years of mortgage paydown together with the equity remaining in the deal cover a fair price for the landowner and leave the non-profit owning both the building and the land beneath it. A clean exit for the contributor; a strong balance sheet for the operator going forward.

**Launchpad cohort.** The first four sites to break ground (2026): 844 Veterans Drive (Barrie · ground-oriented townhomes), Main Street (Listowel · seniors residence, 88 beds), Grahams Lane (Burlington · high-rise), 27 Blake Street (Toronto · mid-rise urban rental). 906 homes total. Deliver by 2028.

**SPV (Special Purpose Vehicle).** The project-level legal entity that holds the contributed land, takes on construction financing, and houses the building. Each site has its own SPV.

**Preferred-equity contribution.** How the landowner's parcel enters the SPV. Not a sale, not a donation. Structured contribution with defined redemption pathway at Year 20.

**CMHC.** Canada Mortgage and Housing Corporation. Federal Crown corporation that provides construction financing for the buildings.

**BCH / Build Canada Homes.** Federal capital alignment programme that flows into Common Ground projects.

## Sentence-level patterns to imitate

- Short sentences for arguments, longer sentences for explanations.
- Em-dashes for asides. Semicolons for parallel structure.
- Numbers in tabular numerals; sites named with their actual address.
- Lists are sparing — prose is preferred.
- Headings are noun phrases ("Four steps. Every site."), not questions or exhortations.
- Italics on key terms or for emphasis; never on entire sentences.
